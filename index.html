üí° Phase 1: Discovery & Strategy

This foundational phase mitigates risk by ensuring you're solving a real problem for a defined audience. It's where an idea is refined into a strategic plan before investing in design or development.

Expert Insight: "The biggest waste in product development is building something nobody wants. The discovery phase is your primary tool to prevent this. Fall in love with the problem, not your solution."

Key Roles (The Visionaries)

  • Product Manager (PM): Owns the product vision, strategy, and roadmap.
  • Business Analyst (BA): Gathers requirements and analyzes market data.
  • UX Researcher: Conducts user interviews and tests to understand user needs.

Essential Skills & Tools

Skills: Market research, strategic thinking, data analysis, competitive analysis.

Tools: SurveyMonkey, Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Aha!, Jira, Miro, Notion.

üé® Phase 2: UI/UX Design

Here, the strategic plan becomes a tangible blueprint. This phase creates a user experience that is visually appealing, logical, intuitive, and accessible, progressing from low-fidelity structure to interactive prototypes.

Expert Insight: "Good design is not just what it looks like and feels like. Good design is how it works. A beautiful app that's confusing is a failed app."

Key Roles (The Architects)

  • UX Designer: Focuses on logic and ease of use. Creates wireframes and user flows.
  • UI Designer: Focuses on the visual look and feel. Creates mockups and prototypes.
  • UX Writer: Crafts clear and concise text to guide the user.

Essential Skills & Tools

Skills: Wireframing, prototyping, information architecture, visual design principles.

Tools: Figma, Sketch, Adobe XD, Balsamiq, Zeplin, InVision.

üíª Phase 3: Development

This is the construction phase where design blueprints are transformed into a functional product. It's divided into the backend (the engine) and the frontend (the interface).

Expert Insight: "Clean code is not a luxury. It's the foundation of a scalable, maintainable, and ultimately successful product. Technical debt always comes due."

Backend Development

  • Role: Backend Developer
  • Goal: Build the server, database, and core application logic.
  • Tech: Node.js, Python, Java, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, AWS, GCP.

Frontend Development

  • Role: Frontend Developer
  • Goal: Translate designs into interactive code.
  • Tech: HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Angular, Vue.js.

üö¢ Phase 5: Deployment & DevOps

This is the launch phase where the tested application is made available to the public. The process differs significantly between web and mobile applications.

Expert Insight: "Amateurs talk about features, professionals talk about deployment pipelines. A smooth, automated release process is a massive competitive advantage."

üîÑ Phase 6: Maintenance & Iteration

Launch is the beginning of the most important feedback loop. This phase involves monitoring the live application, gathering user feedback, and using that data to continuously improve the product.

Expert Insight: "The most successful products aren't just built; they evolve. The 'Build-Measure-Learn' loop isn't a startup fad, it's the fundamental engine of sustainable growth."

Key Roles (The Growth Team)

  • Product Manager: Analyzes user data to prioritize future development.
  • Data Analyst: Uncovers insights from user behavior data.
  • Marketing Manager: Works to acquire and retain users.

Essential Skills & Tools

Skills: SQL, statistical analysis, data visualization, A/B testing, SEO/SEM.

Tools: Mixpanel, Amplitude, Google Analytics, Tableau, Intercom.
